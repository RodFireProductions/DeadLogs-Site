About

The creating of DeadJournals honestly just came out of a whim.

For awhile I had been thinking of a good way to format and display blog like posts for my personal website. My main site is currently run using Jekyll but even with Jekyll's convience, it just didn't seem write for me to actually makes posts on it. I think it's the idea of needing to make my site look professional and clean that's making things seems uneasy to me.

The push to create DeadJournals was out of a need of mine to make my own kind of templating code. Jekyll is nice, I won't deny that, but I'm truly not sure if it's for me, definitely not if I wish to blog/devlog about my work. One thing about it that really pushes me away is it wanting you to write your posts in markdown. Of course, you don't have to but you'll end up mainly dealing with markdown files anyways and it's a turn off for me.

One night my brain told me I should finally trying making my own blogging system ended up jumping head first into it without really knowing what I was doing, but I soon got the hang of it. In the middle of my development, the idea hit me to not just make this a personal blog, but to make it into a tool that can be easily reused when needed. I figured it'd be nice to make this tool public as well for anyone who was in need of a less complicated and intimidating static site template system.

Plus, this is great webdevelopment practice for me. I've already learned a lot so far when it comes to dealing with HTML DOM in Javascript.

- Rodrick
